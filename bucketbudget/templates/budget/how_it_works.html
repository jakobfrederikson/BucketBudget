A budgeting technique from The Barefoot Investor, by Scott Pape

Bucket Budget is a tool to help you automate your budget and collaborate with others you might share your budget with.

The Barefoot Investor

You can check out the author's website for the book here. This website/calculator is just a tool to help you get up and running with your budget and to collaborate with others if need be. It is by no means an in-depth guide of what Scott Pape teaches through his book.

How Bucket Budget works
  1. Create: Create a budget and decide if you're budgeting weekly, fortnightly, four-weekly, monthly or yearly.
  2. Income: Enter your total take-home pay.
  3. Expenses: Enter any expenses you have.
  4. Buckets: Name your buckets and decide what percentage of your income should be allocated towards each bucket.
  5. Not sure what buckets to create? Then use this template to start:
    • Daily Expenses (60%): An everyday account used for groceries, travel, or any other non-negotiable expense.
    • Splurge (10%): For fun things like coffee, online shopping, or a night out.
    • Smile (10%): For long term savings goals like holidays, or even a PS5.
    • Fire Extinguisher (20%): To put out a 'financial fire', like any debt.
  6. Ensure your buckets total to 100%: Make sure that every piece of your income is mapped to a bucket so that you don't have money sitting in your bank account doing nothing.
  7. Budget Result: Use the budget result calculated by Bucket Budget to set up automatic payments in your banking app so that you don't have to worry about your budget anymore!
  8. Invite others: If you're saving with a friend, partner, or family-member, add them to the budget so they can view and collaborate on the budget with you.
Additional Information
  • Bucket Budget does not connect to your banking app or cost money. It is simply a tool that does the percentage based 'bucket calculations' for you.
  • With Bucket Budget, you can mark expenses as 'expense buckets' if they're important and you would like to make sure you always have money for them. Have a monthly internet bill of $100 that you always want to make sure you can pay? Then mark it as an expense bucket.
Only mark expenses as a bucket if it requires its own account for saving. Every other expense item should just automatically flow out of your main spending account.
